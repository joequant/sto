What lawyers do

  • Lawyers keep you out of trouble
  • They check for compliance against the law
  • They help you work through parts of the law that are complicated or counterinitutive
  • They are messengers of the state (don't do this or bad things will happen)

The lawyer view of the world

  • Very risk averse
  • Very local based
  • Mostly pulled into bad situations. i.e. your average divorce lawyer isn't involved with happy marriages

Limits with the lawyer view of the world

  • The lawyer usually tells you what you can't do
  • The lawyer can't tell you about other jurisdictions
  • The lawyer tells you what the law is, not what it should be and often not what it will be
  • Sometimes the answer is "I don't know"

Investment banker and theoretical physicist views of the world

  • Investment bankers make deals. Deals are usually based on human emotion. Namely fear and greed. Emotions are universal across human beings, and also you are dealing with someone that wants to deal.
  • Theoretical physicist hat. Look for universals. Figure out theories of behavior

Thought experiment

  • I go into a store in France. I take a candy bar. I pay. Everything works ok.
  • I go into a store in France. I take a candy bar. I then take off my pants and punch the store owner in the face.
  • I think I will get into trouble. How do I know that I've done something bad even though I know nothing about the French legal code.

Law is like food

  • Different places have different ways of approach law and finance just like different places have different foods
  • There are some universals. Arsenic in food is bad. Don't leave meat out or it will spoil.
  • But different places have different approaches to money.

Why are securities special?

  • I buy a candy bar. I give money. The person at the other end gives me a candy bar. If they just take my money and don't give me a candy bar, I know something went wrong.
  • I buy a security. I hand the seller money, and he shakes my hand, gives me some papers, and walks away. How do I know that he didn't just steal my money?
    • It gets worse. It's *easier* for someone to take the money and run, than to actually do something real. You make *more* money if you take the money and run.
    • Also suppose you catch the guy and toss them in jail. I'm still out of my money. I don't care if the guy is in jail. I'm still lost my money.

What's wrong with securities laws?

  • Securities laws are based on access control by national governments. We make sure that you can't get exposed to bad stuff.
  • This will not work with the internet unless you want to close your borders
  • Securities laws fix some problems but lead to others.

The analogy with food

  • If you go to a fast food restaurant in the US, you are very unlikely to get sick because of food poisoning. Health and safety insures that. However, if you eat the US diet, you are likely to die from heart disease or diabetes.
  • By contrast if you go to somewhere with weak health inspections, you find ways of avoiding getting sick, and often the diet is healthier.
